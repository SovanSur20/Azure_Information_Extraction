MACHINING NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. All dimensions in millimeters unless otherwise specified
2. Remove all burrs and sharp edges, max 0.2 mm chamfer
3. Protect threads during machining and handling
4. Maintain concentricity within 0.02 mm TIR
5. Grind bearing surfaces after heat treatment
6. Cold roll threads for improved fatigue resistance
7. Mark part number on non-critical surface (laser etch)
8. Clean and degrease before passivation


ASSEMBLY NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Install key before coupling assembly
2. Apply anti-seize compound to threads
3. Torque thread nut to 450 Nm ±25 Nm
4. Check runout after assembly (max 0.08 mm)
5. Balance to ISO 1940 Grade G2.5
6. Protect bearing surfaces during storage
